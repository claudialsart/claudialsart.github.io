What I Paint

I create what I cannot explain in words — emotions that echo like gravity, stillness that feels infinite. Through oil painting, I explore the quiet drama of space and the intimate turbulence of human feeling. My style moves between realism and abstraction, between cosmos and closeness. I call it abstract emotional realism — a visual language of tenderness, silence, and survival.

My Journey

I’m a self-taught artist. I didn’t follow a traditional path, I carved one. My art is born from solitude, deep reflection, and a personal transformation sparked by motherhood. After losing nearly everything: my home, my studio, my sense of direction. I began again. Painting became a way to hold onto something real, something I could control, shape, and pour myself into. It still is.

Where I Am Now

Right now, I’m rebuilding: my body of work, my studio, and my visibility. I don’t have a team. I don’t post daily. I’m not part of a gallery yet. But I’m here, painting at night while my baby sleeps, showing up in quiet, persistent ways. I currently accept commissions and create one-of-a-kind oil paintings from a small home space in London. Every piece is part of this slow, powerful comeback. These aren’t just paintings. They’re fragments of survival, hope, and the cosmos inside me.
